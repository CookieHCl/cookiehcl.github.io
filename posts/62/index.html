Motivation

Implementing curves and surfaces with lots of points is inefficient.
We want higher-level representation of curves and surfaces.
Specifically, we would like to find a smooth curve/surface with a minimal number of control points.

Parametric Geometry

p(t)=(x(t),y(t))p(t) = (x(t), y(t)) p(t)=(x(t),y(t))

p(u,v)=(x(u,v),y(u,v),z(u,v))p(u, v) = (x(u, v), y(u, v), z(u, v)) p(u,v)=(x(u,v),y(u,v),z(u,v))

e.g. parameterized line passing through (t0,p0),(t1,p1)(t_0, p_0), (t_1, p_1)(t0​,p0​),(t1​,p1​) is

Why parameteric?

It matches the intrinsic dimension of objects we want to manipulate.
Curves are 1D, surfaces are 2D.
It may exist in higher dimensions, but it doesn't matter!

Also, once a curve/surface is parameterized, sampling process is easy, so our graphics hardware can render easily.
We can sample like P(0),P(0.1),P(0.2),…P(0), P(0.1), P(0.2), \ldotsP(0),P(0.1),P(0.2),…

Differential Properties of Parameteric Curves

Velocity

Instantaneous positional change: P′(t)P'(t)P′(t)

Tangent

Normalized velocity vector: T(t)≔P′(t)∥P′(t)∥T(t) \coloneqq \frac{P'(t)}{\|{P'(t)}\|}T(t):=∥P′(t)∥P′(t)​

Curvature

Instantaneous tangential change: K(T)≔T′(t)K(T) \coloneqq T'(t)K(T):=T′(t)
Curvature is always orthogonal to tangent, K(t)⋅T(t)=0K(t) \cdot T(t) = 0K(t)⋅T(t)=0

In geometry, 1∥K(t)∥\frac{1}{\|K(t)\|}∥K(t)∥1​ is same as the radius of the circle that touches P(t)P(t)P(t) at ttt.

Curve Normal

Normalized curvature vector: N(t)≔K(t)∥K(t)∥N(t) \coloneqq \frac{K(t)}{\|{K(t)}\|}N(t):=∥K(t)∥K(t)​

In 3D, there is also a binormal vector: B(t)≔T(t)×N(t)B(t) \coloneqq T(t) \times N(t)B(t):=T(t)×N(t)

Tangent vector, Curve normal vector, Binormal vector form a coordinate system called Frenet frame.
However, it is not well defined (curvature can be 0).

  • End-point interpolation. x(0)=x0,x(1)=x3x(0) = x_0, x(1) = x_3x(0)=x0​,x(1)=x3​

Spline

A piecewise polynomial with a high degree of smoothness where pieces meet.
Use multiple curves to make complex curve!

e.g. TrueType fonts use quadratic bezier curve, and PostScript fonts use cubic bezier curve.

Continuity

To ensure a smooth transition from one section of a piecewise parametric spline to the next, we can impose various continuity conditions at the connection points.

  • Parametric continuity: Matching the parametric derivatives of adjoining curve sections at their common boundary
  • Geometric continuity: Geometric smoothness independent of parametrization (i.e. parametrization can be different between curves!)

Orders of Continuity

  • C0C^0C0, G0G^0G0 continuity (positional continuity): The positions of common control points are the same
  • G1G^1G1 continuity (tangency continuity): C0C^0C0 and the directions of derivates are the same
  • C1C^1C1 continuity: C0C^0C0 and the derivatives are the same
  • G2G^2G2 continuity (curvature continuity): G1G^1G1 and the second derivatives (or curvature) are the same
  • C2C^2C2 continuity: C1C^1C1 and the second derivatives (or curvature) are the same

G2G^2G2, C2C^2C2 continuity is extremely hard...

e.g. When connecting cubic bezier curves,

  • C0C^0C0 continuity is guaranteed if points are same.
  • G1G^1G1 continuity is guaranteed if three control points are on a straight line.
  • C1C^1C1 continuity is guaranteed if three control points are on a straight line, and the distance is the same. (recall: bezier curve's derivative is 3 times difference between control points.)

c.f. Fonts someimtes need to be angled.
Solution: Overlap control points!
By overlapping two control points, we lose C1C^1C1 continuity, but we get angled curve.

Bezier Splines with Tangent Conditions

Bezier curve is tedious - we need to position every control points carefully.

Instead, just give points and tangent vectors of each points.
Then we can poisition control points by moving 1/3 of the tangent vector.

Catmull-Rom Splines

Giving tangent vectors is tedious - just give points and calculate tangent vectors!
First and last tangents are set to be zero, and other tangents are set to difference between adjacent points.

p0′=pn′=0,pi′=pi+1−pi−12\mathbf{p}'_0 = \mathbf{p}'_n = 0, \mathbf{p}'_i = \frac{\mathbf{p}_{i+1} - \mathbf{p}_{i - 1}}{2} p0′​=pn′​=0,pi′​=2pi+1​−pi−1​​

Only points are needed to create C1C^1C1 continuous curve that passes through them!
We can either position control points and draw a Bezier spline, or draw a Hermite spline directly.

Natural Cubic Splines

Is C1C^1C1 enough? Car surface have to guarantee at least G3G^3G3 continuity.
Theoretically, splines of degree n can guarantee Cn−1C^{n-1}Cn−1 continuity.

Given n + 1 control points, we want to find n connected Bezier curves passing through the points with C2C^2C2 continuity.

  • We have 4n unknowns (4 control points per each curve)
  • We have 4n-2 equations
    • 2n equations for end point interpolations
    • n-1 equations for tangential continuity
    • n-1 equations for second derivative continuity

We need 2 more equations! We need to specify boundary conditions.

  • For open curve, p′′(t0)=p′′(tn)=0p''(t_0) = p''(t_n) = 0p′′(t0​)=p′′(tn​)=0 (i.e., curve ends with a straight line)
  • For closed curve, p′(t0)=p′(tn)p'(t_0) = p'(t_n)p′(t0​)=p′(tn​) and p′′(t0)=p′′(tn)p''(t_0) = p''(t_n)p′′(t0​)=p′′(tn​)

Natural Cubic Splines is affine-invariant!
But we lose local controllability, i.e. moving one point affects the entire curve.

B-splines

Motivation: We need C2C^2C2 continuous curve with local controllability.

Use all four consecutive points as control points.
e.g. p0,p1,p2,p3p_0, p_1, p_2, p_3p0​,p1​,p2​,p3​ forms control points, p1,p2,p3,p4p_1, p_2, p_3, p_4p1​,p2​,p3​,p4​ forms control points, ...

We get local controllability, but we lose interpolation, i.e. curves no longer pass through any points.

Curve segment is changed when ttt becomes an integer.
Since curve segment is changed uniformly, (i.e. knots are equally spaced) this spline is called uniform cubic B-spline.

Properties of B-splines

  • Convex hull
  • Affine invariance
  • Cn−1C^{n-1}Cn−1 continuity
  • Local controllability
  • Variation diminishing: B-spline never crosses any given plane more often than its control polygon does.
